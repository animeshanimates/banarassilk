Since 1924

Four generations.
One loom, unbroken.

Banaras Silk Mfg. Co. was founded in 1924 on the banks of the Ganges, in a city whose weavers have shaped the world's understanding of fine silk for over a thousand years. What began as a single family workshop has grown, across four generations, into a Star Export House trusted by importers, buying houses and luxury brands across Europe, North America and East Asia.

We remain, deliberately, a manufacturer, not a marketplace. Every metre we produce is developed, woven, finished and inspected under one roof in Varanasi, combining traditional handloom knowledge with modern production discipline, so that our partners can build collections with confidence.

Manufacturing

From raw fibre to export crate.

Twelve stages, refined over a century, carried out entirely within our own facilities in Varanasi.

01

Raw Material

Natural fibres (mulberry silk, cotton, linen) sourced and graded for consistency before entering production.

02

Yarn Selection

Yarns are selected and tested for count, twist and strength to match the intended weave and end use.

03

Warping

Thousands of warp threads are aligned with precision, setting the foundation for every metre woven.

04

Handloom

Skilled karigars weave fine and complex constructions by hand, preserving techniques unique to Banaras.

05

Powerloom

Modern looms produce volume fabrics with the consistency export orders demand, without sacrificing hand-feel.

06

Dyeing

Colour is matched to international standards and tested for fastness across light, wash and rub.

07

Printing

Traditional and digital printing techniques bring pattern and detail to base cloth with exacting registration.

08

Embroidery

Hand and machine embroidery add texture and value, from fine zari work to contemporary embellishment.

09

Finishing

Washing, calendaring and specialist finishes prepare fabric for its final application and market.

10

Inspection

Every roll passes through multi-point quality inspection against buyer-specific AQL standards.

11

Packing

Goods are packed to destination-market specification, protecting fabric integrity in transit.

12

Export

Documentation, compliance and logistics are managed in-house for reliable delivery worldwide.

Why Indian Silk

The fibre that travelled the world before trade routes had names.

Indian silk, and Banaras silk in particular, has been documented and admired by travellers and historians for centuries, carried along the Silk Route, sought after in the courts of Europe and Central Asia, and prized for a lustre and hand-feel that natural mulberry, tussar, eri and muga silks are still known for today.

That reputation was not built on marketing. It was built on generations of weavers who understood fibre, tension and finish in a way no machine has fully replicated. It is the same knowledge our looms draw on today.

Quality Assurance

Consistency, verified at every stage.

Quality is not a final check. It is designed into every stage of our process, from yarn selection through to the last inspection before packing. Our systems are built to meet the AQL standards, testing protocols and documentation that international buyers require.

  • 01

    In-Process Inspection

    Fabric is checked at every production stage, not only at the end, so faults are caught before value is added.

  • 02

    Lab Testing

    Colour fastness, shrinkage, tensile strength and composition are tested against buyer and international standards.

  • 03

    AQL-Based Final Inspection

    Finished goods are inspected to agreed AQL levels prior to packing, with reports available on request.

  • 04

    Consistency Across Runs

    Documented processes and trained supervisors keep quality consistent between sample and bulk production.
