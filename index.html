Since 1924

Five generations.
One loom, unbroken.

Established in 1924, Banaras Silk Mfg. Co. is one of India's oldest and most respected textile manufacturers, carrying forward a five-generation family legacy in the art of handwoven and handloom fabrics. Founded originally as the Banaras Brocade Museum, the company quickly became synonymous with the finest handwoven silk brocades from the historic city of Varanasi, and once operated a network of 22 retail stores across India's hill stations, weaving with real gold and silver zari for royal and aristocratic households.

An invitation to showcase our work in Great Britain marked the beginning of our export journey. Today, that journey spans more than 55 countries, with the company continuing to blend traditional weaving heritage with contemporary textile innovation across Silk, Cotton, Linen and blended fabrics, under one roof in Varanasi, so our partners can build collections with confidence.

Manufacturing

From raw fibre to export crate.

Ten stages, refined over a century, carried out entirely within our own facilities in Varanasi.

01

Yarn Procurement

Natural fibres and yarns (mulberry silk, cotton, linen) are sourced and graded for consistency before entering production.

02

Dyeing

Yarn is cleaned, treated and dyed to match international colour standards and tested for fastness.

03

Handloom Weaving

Skilled karigars weave fine and complex constructions by hand, preserving techniques unique to Banaras, including intricate brocades.

04

Power Loom Weaving

Modern looms produce volume fabrics with the consistency export orders demand, without sacrificing hand-feel.

05

Printing

Traditional and digital printing techniques bring pattern and detail to base cloth with exacting registration.

06

Embroidery

Hand and machine embroidery add texture and value, from fine zari work to contemporary embellishment.

07

Finishing

Washing, calendaring, polishing and softening prepare fabric for its final application and market.

08

Stitching

Made-ups and apparel are cut and stitched to specification for finished, ready-to-ship products.

09

Quality Control

Every piece passes through multi-point quality inspection for weave, colour consistency, defects and measurements against buyer-specific AQL standards.

10

Packaging

Goods are measured, folded or rolled, labelled and packed to destination-market specification for dispatch worldwide.

Why Indian Silk

The fibre that travelled the world before trade routes had names.

Indian silk, and Banaras silk in particular, has been documented and admired by travellers and historians for centuries, carried along the Silk Route, sought after in the courts of Europe and Central Asia, and prized for a lustre and hand-feel that natural mulberry, tussar, eri and muga silks are still known for today.

That reputation was not built on marketing. It was built on generations of weavers who understood fibre, tension and finish in a way no machine has fully replicated. The same craftsmanship that earned our family an invitation to showcase Indian silk brocades in Great Britain, opening the doors to the global markets we serve today.

Quality Assurance

Consistency, verified at every stage.

Quality is not a final check. It is designed into every stage of our process, from yarn selection through to the last inspection before packing. Our systems are built to meet the AQL standards, testing protocols and documentation that international buyers require.

  • 01

    In-Process Inspection

    Fabric is checked at every production stage, not only at the end, so faults are caught before value is added.

  • 02

    Lab Testing

    Colour fastness, shrinkage, tensile strength and composition are tested against buyer and international standards.

  • 03

    AQL-Based Final Inspection

    Finished goods are inspected to agreed AQL levels prior to packing, with reports available on request.

  • 04

    Consistency Across Runs

    Documented processes and trained supervisors keep quality consistent between sample and bulk production.
